Air Quality Precautions

Particulate Matter (PM) Sensor Range for Pollution: 60ug/m3 above Possible Causes/Risks: may arise due tovehicle emissions, industrial processes, construction activities, and burning of fossil fuels. Safety Precautions: Regular maintenance and calibration of the sensor are essential.

Gas Sensor Range for Pollution: Depends on the specific gas being measured (e.g., carbon monoxide, nitrogen dioxide, sulfur dioxide). Possible Causes/Risks: may arise due to vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, and other combustion processes. Safety Precautions: Ensure proper ventilation and installation of the sensor.

ALERT: Increase in all sensors advices that all residents must wear facemask.

Water Quality Precautions

Conductivity Sensor: 600 mg/L above Possible Causes/Risks: may arise due to industrial discharges, agricultural runoff, or other sources introducing ions into the water. Safety Precautions: Proper calibration and maintenance are essential. Ensure proper handling of chemicals.

pH Sensor: 6.5 to 8.5 may indicate pollution Possible Causes/Risks: may arise due to acid rain, industrial discharges, or agricultural runoff. Safety Precautions: Calibrate the pH sensor regularly. Ensure proper handling of chemicals.

Turbidity Sensor: 50 mg/L (Above) may indicate pollution. Possible Causes/Risks: may arise due to soil erosion, industrial discharges, or other suspended particles in water. Safety Precautions: Regular calibration and maintenance. Ensure proper handling of chemicals if needed for calibration.

ALERT: Increase in all sensors advices that all residents must avoid water until the range of the sensors go back into safety range.

Parameters

Air Sensors Standard Level/s

Particulate Matter: 50 µg/m3

Gas Sensor: between 400 - 1000ppm

Ozone Sensor: between 10ppm and 1000ppm or 60 µg/m3

Water Sensors Standard Level/s

Conductivity Sensor : 500 mg/L

pH Sensor: 6.5 - 8.5

Turbidity Sensor: 50mg/L
